Medgar Evers

Growing up

After serving in the US Army during World War II he attended Alcorn College and graduated as an insurance salesman and started getting involved in the civil rights movement.

Read more
Education

Evers served for the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) in the 1950s and early 1960s as the first field secretary. He worked to register black voters, investigate lynchings, and organize boycotts of businesses that discriminated against black people. He also fought against cruel Jim Crow laws, protested segregation in education, and launched an investigation into the Emmett Till lynching. Until his murder on June 12, 1963.

Impact

His assassination was a major catalyst for the Civil Rights Movement. He is remembered as a hero and martyr of the movement. His murdered, Byron De La Beckwith, was convicted 31 years later, in 1994.
